Late Blight

Brown spots grow from leaf margin, white covering on the underside of leaves, gray or brown wrinkled stains on fruits, hardened fruit flesh and fruit decay.Biological control: Remove and destroy plant around infected spot. Do not compost infected part. Chemical control: Fungicide sprays based on mandopropamid, chlorothelonil, fluazinum, triphenyltin, mancozeb.

Nitrogen deficiency

Older mature leaf turn pale green, with red discoloration on petioles and veins. Later they become uniformly yellow first and later yellowish white. Young leaves remain pale green and grow shorter than usual. Plant have spindly aspect with normal height.Chemical control: High level of organic matter in soil like manure, compost, etc. Chemical control: Use of products having ammonium, urea and nitrate

Tomato yellow leaf curl virus

Thicker and wrinkled leaves with interveinal chlorosis clearly visible on the leaf blade. Number of fruits reduced. Chemical Control: Use a neonicotinoid insecticide, such as dinotefuran (Venom) imidacloprid (AdmirePro, Alias, Nuprid, Widow, and others) or thiamethoxam (Platinum)

Early blight on tomato

Dark spots on leaves, dark concentric circles on stem and fruit. Biological control: Spray algal limestone or rock flour. Chemical control: Fungicides containing copper, mancozeb, chlorothalonil, azoxystrobin, pyraclostrobin, trifloxystrobin, fenamidone and famoxidone.

Potassium deficiency

Leaf tip burn, white spots. Biological control: Animal manure or plant mulch or wood ash. Chemical control: Potassium chloride, potassium nitrate, potassium sulphate and monopotassium phosphate.

Magnesium deficiency

Mottled chlorotic areas on leaves, later yellowing engulfs the leaves. Biological control: Manure, organic mulches or compost. Chemical control: Foliar fertilizers containing magnesium components, magnesium oxide, magnesium sulphate.
